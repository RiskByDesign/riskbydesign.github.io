Privacy policy

Last updated 17 July 2026 · Applies to BowCRQ desktop and Android, version 0.8.0

BowCRQ is a local-first application, built so that your data stays on your device. This policy states, precisely and checkably, what data exists, where it lives, and the single case in which anything can leave your device. It mirrors the technical guarantees in the security model; where this policy summarises, that document is the detail.

Who is responsible

BowCRQ is developed independently under the RiskByDesign name. There is no company behind it, no server operated on your behalf, and no account for you held anywhere but on your own device.

The short version

  • BowCRQ collects nothing about you. No telemetry, analytics, usage statistics, crash reporting, advertising or tracking of any kind.
  • Everything you create stays in a local database on your device. The developer never receives it.
  • Nothing leaves your device except in one case you control: the optional AI review feature, which is off by default and sends data to your own AI provider only after you enable it and supply your own key.
  • There is no cloud account. Passwords never leave the device and cannot be recovered.

Data you create, and where it lives

Everything you build in BowCRQ is stored in a single SQLite database in your per-user application data directory:

  • Bow tie models: nodes, connections, calibrations and settings.
  • The control registry and its assurance history.
  • Saved simulation runs and version snapshots.
  • The observation register: qualitative findings, notes, sources and tags.
  • The evidence register: your own incident data and cited external sources, with their data points, provenance (source, version, URL, date) and credibility notes.
  • The audit trail of governed, evidence-informed changes.
  • Your local account (username and a hashed password).

None of this is transmitted anywhere by the application. It exists only on your device until you delete it.

Information BowCRQ does not collect

  • No telemetry, analytics or usage statistics.
  • No crash or error reporting.
  • No update checks or other automatic "phone home" requests.
  • No advertising identifiers, device fingerprinting or location data.
  • No access to your contacts, camera or microphone.
  • No email address or other contact detail (accounts are a username and password only).

The one network exception: optional AI review

BowCRQ ships an optional AI assistant that can review a bow tie or help draft a report. It is strictly opt-in:

  • It is off by default and does nothing until you enable it and paste your own AI provider API key (currently Anthropic). The key is stored locally on your device.
  • When you enable it and request a review, BowCRQ sends only the content that review needs over HTTPS to your provider (https://api.anthropic.com): a compact text description of the bow tie (node names and kinds, linked control names, and the connections between nodes) and, for a report, a summary of the simulation result metrics. It does not send your database, your evidence register notes, your sources, your credibility notes or your account details.
  • The application's Content Security Policy permits network connections to that single host and no other, so even this feature cannot talk to anywhere else.
  • Once data reaches your chosen provider it is handled under that provider's own privacy terms, not this policy. Review your provider's terms before enabling the feature. To keep BowCRQ fully offline, simply leave the feature disabled or do not supply a key.

Authentication and biometrics

  • Accounts are local to the device: a username and a password only. Passwords are hashed with Argon2id and there is no recovery mechanism by design, so a forgotten password means a lost account.
  • Sessions are held in memory and end when the app closes.
  • On Android, optional biometric unlock is handled by the operating system. BowCRQ never sees or stores your fingerprint or any biometric data; it only holds a local, hashed device token that the OS biometric prompt unlocks. See the security model for the detail.

Data sharing

  • The developer shares none of your data, because the developer receives none of it.
  • The only third party that can ever receive any of your data is the AI provider you choose, and only through the opt-in feature described above.
  • The app is built with third-party open-source software components, listed exhaustively in the SBOM and summarised in the third-party notices. These are libraries compiled into the application; they run locally and do not receive or transmit your data.

Exports and shared files

BowCRQ can export bow ties, the observation register and the evidence register as plain JSON files, and can produce reports. These files are not encrypted and contain whatever you put into the model. Anyone you send an export or report to receives its full contents, so handle them with the same care as the database itself.

Data at rest and security

The local database is not separately encrypted; confidentiality at rest is provided by your platform (BitLocker or equivalent on Windows, file-based encryption on Android). Anyone with full filesystem access to an unlocked, unencrypted device can read the data. Use device encryption. The full security model, including at-rest protection and the governed audit trail, is documented in the security model.

Retention and deletion

  • You control retention entirely. Data persists until you delete it in the app or remove the application's data.
  • Deleting a bow tie or a control deliberately returns any attached observations and evidence to their registers rather than destroying those findings; delete those items explicitly if you want them gone.
  • Uninstalling the app, or deleting its data directory, removes the database. There are no server-side copies anywhere to request deletion of, because there is no server.

Children

BowCRQ is a professional risk-analysis tool, not directed at children, and it collects no personal information from anyone.

Your control over your data

Because all of your data is local and self-hosted, you have direct and complete control over it: you can access, export, correct and delete it at any time, inside the app or by managing its files. There is no external data controller holding your information, so there is no one to make a subject-access or deletion request to; the controls are all in your hands.

Changes to this policy

This policy is versioned with the application. The "last updated" date and the applicable version at the top indicate its currency; material changes will update both.

Contact

BowCRQ is a personal project without a formal support channel. Questions about this policy or the app's data handling can be raised with the maintainer directly at cyber@riskbydesign.net, as noted in the security model.

This policy describes the application's actual behaviour, which is verifiable from the source, the SBOM and the security model. It is provided in good faith and is not legal advice; if you are adopting BowCRQ within an organisation that has specific regulatory obligations, have it reviewed against those obligations.
